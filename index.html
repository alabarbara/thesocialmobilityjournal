Instructions:

-Prompt: Connect the dots between the influences in your life that have shaped you. Tell us what makes you resilient. (500-600 words)
- Make your journal entry personal, analytical, or both, as long as it is nonfiction.
- You may use A.I. to help edit, but the story must be written by you.
-Submit your writing at the appropriate link above.
- Powerful stories will advance to a final round of edits and publishing.
- It doesn't have to be perfect, we want to hear your voice.



Writers are evaluated by:

1. Creative Storytelling
2. Critical Thinking
3 Resilience



Winners receive:

1. The opportunity to publish personal stories or research on SMJ
2. Access to a national network of investigative journalists
3. A mystery surprise!



DISCLAIMER:

It is SMJ's highest priority to protect the privacy of our writers, thus, writers may choose to remain anonymous (under a pseudonym or first name) or publish their work with their real name. No identifying information beyond the student's name will be published. Writers may request to withdraw their work from SMJ at any point by submitting the Request for Entry Withdrawl Form.

GUIDELINES:

1. Make a copy of the submission template.
2. Input requested personal information, the prompt for the application, and the essay draft.
3. Choose whether you want 1 round of edits or 2 rounds.
4. Within 2-3 weeks of submission, the writer will receive an email with edit suggestions. For those choosing 1 round of edits, this edited version will be published on SMJ with a title and thumbnail.
5. For those who chose 2 rounds of edits, the writer will resubmit the edited version and receive a second round of suggestions within 2-3 weeks. This edited version will be published on SMJ.

About Us:

The Social Mobility Journal is a nonprofit publication that empowers youth journalism to document the lived reality of American working-class families. Through national writing competitions and workshops, SMJ offers a platform for silenced voices to be uplifted. 100% authentically human stories, unforgettable resilience.

Our Goal:

To expand nationally with paid competitions and writing workshops at 500 educational institutions by 2030.
